Problem Statement

Drivers need to make extra detours while the location of passenger is with lower spatial accessibility. And when it comes to multiple passengers in a single ride, the cost of the extra detours would become less acceptable.


The flexible pickup and drop-off points are able to reduce time cost and transportation anxiety by avoiding detours and heavy traffic sections, but not all the nearby locations can have positive effects on driving routs.


Objective

To design a reality-based approach to determine the optimal pickup and drop-off points that can effectively improve the driving rout in urban ridesharing.

Conclusion

Conclusion

The result presented that time, distance, detour, sections and clear rate of roads of the whole shared ride could be reduced if the passengers chose the PDPs provided by the proposed approach, however the for an individual trip for each passengers, the reduction in time-cost could not be guaranteed .
